With a diverse range of professional roles spanning from barn management and equestrian instruction to laboratory research in entomology, my personal work experiences reflect a passion for hands-on learning and a commitment to contributing meaningfully to each field I enter. In my role as a Laboratory Research Assistant in the Entomology Department at the University of California Riverside, I provide crucial support to graduate student research projects. A significant portion of my responsibilities involves hands-on work with maintaining whitefly colonies and ensuring the optimal health and growth of associated plants. This experience has afforded me a comprehensive understanding of insect behavior and plant-insect interactions, key components of entomological research. Moreover, I have gained proficiency in a wide range of laboratory techniques, contributing to the overall success of our research endeavors. My dedication to precision and my ability to collaborate effectively within a team have been instrumental in advancing our understanding of entomology and its applications in agricultural and environmental sciences.

As a Research Assistant at Nippon Veterinary and Life Science University, I contributed to various research projects focusing on shelter medicine, animal abuse, and animal welfare. My responsibilities included documenting necropsy findings from cases of animal abuse, conducting fieldwork to gather data on different animal shelters and animal hoarding situations across Japan, and assisting in data analysis and report preparation. This role afforded me a comprehensive understanding of the complexities surrounding animal welfare issues and equipped me with practical research skills applicable to addressing these challenges.

As Barn Manager at Sterling Riding Academy, I oversaw the daily operations of a 20-stall facility, including two outside paddocks and a 20-acre pasture. My responsibilities included managing a team of up to six stable patrons and students, coordinating barn maintenance, and overseeing equipment management. I ensured the timely provision of essential services such as horse shoeing, veterinary care, and feeding. Through effective leadership and organization, I maintained a high standard of care for our equine residents while fostering a positive environment for staff and clients. In my role as a Riding Instructor at Sterling Riding Academy, I had the privilege of guiding children aged 6-16 in developing fundamental to advanced equine skills for both pleasure and competitive riding. I designed and implemented tailored lesson plans to cater to varying skill levels, focusing on disciplines such as equitation, flatwork, hunter, and jumping. My teaching extended beyond technical proficiency to encompass values of sportsmanship, discipline, and respect for our equine partners.
